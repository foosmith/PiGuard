# PiGuard Privacy Policy

Last updated: April 6, 2026

PiGuard is a macOS menu bar application for connecting to and managing user-configured DNS filtering servers such as Pi-hole and AdGuard Home.

## Summary

PiGuard does not operate a developer-hosted cloud service for app accounts, analytics, advertising, or remote data collection.

PiGuard connects directly from your Mac to the servers that you configure inside the app.

## Information PiGuard Uses

PiGuard may locally store the following information on your device:

- server hostnames or IP addresses
- server ports and protocol choices
- usernames
- passwords, API tokens, or app passwords used to connect to your servers
- app preferences such as polling rate, sync settings, and display options
- optional local diagnostic logs when logging is enabled

This information is used only to provide the app’s functionality on your device.

## Network Connections

PiGuard sends requests only to the servers you configure, such as:

- Pi-hole servers
- AdGuard Home servers

These network requests are used to:

- test connectivity
- fetch server status and statistics
- toggle blocking or protection
- refresh gravity or filter data
- load query log and top-item data
- perform Pi-hole v6 sync operations when configured

PiGuard does not send this server data to a PiGuard-operated backend service.

## Analytics and Tracking

PiGuard does not include advertising SDKs.

PiGuard does not use third-party analytics or user tracking.

PiGuard does not collect data for cross-app tracking.

## Crash Reporting

PiGuard does not include a third-party crash reporting service.

Apple may process crash or diagnostic information according to its own platform policies if you choose to share that information with Apple.

## Local Logging

PiGuard includes optional local diagnostic logging for troubleshooting.

If enabled, logs are stored locally on your Mac. These logs are not automatically transmitted to the developer.

## Data Sharing

PiGuard does not sell your personal information.

PiGuard does not share your information with data brokers or advertising networks.

## Data Retention

Data stored by PiGuard remains on your device unless you remove it, uninstall the app, or change your configured servers and settings.

## Security Note

PiGuard currently stores some connection credentials locally as part of app preferences on device. The app does not currently rely on a remote account system.

## Your Choices

You can:

- remove configured servers from the app
- disable local diagnostic logging
- uninstall the app and delete its local data
